## Our Purpose

Ahmedabad University  is dedicated to foster continuous progress of self and society. We aspire to transform higher education and research in India. Our graduates have the capabilities, attitudes, and values to excel in any field they pursue around the world.

- To prepare leaders of outstanding character who will contribute significantly to their fields of study and practice
- To build an evolving learning environment that is based on cross-disciplinary linkages between the arts, sciences, and professional disciplines, combined with rigour and reflection
- To actively advance research and intellectual enterprise at the University and in society
- To promote independent-mindedness and diversity across all dimensions of the University
- To generate knowledge that provides a context of learning from and contribution to India and the world
- To  actively  advance  the  social,  economic,  and  ecological  development  of  local,  national,  and  international communities
- To educate young people of India and the world to become contextually-literate global citizens
- To  catalyse  students  to  mature  into  critical  thinkers  who  are  analytically  equipped,  practically  oriented,  and ethically driven

We recognise that social challenges and job opportunities are occurring at the intersection of various axes of influence, defined by disciplines (data, materials, biology, and behaviour), nature (air, water, forests, and land), sectors of impact (health, transport, energy, and education) and society (individual and community). Accordingly, Ahmedabad University strives to guide students on how to learn through interdisciplinary academics and real-life experiences that traverse these intersections. The research programmes at the University also embody this integrative philosophy.

## A Brief History

Ahmedabad University  was  founded  by  the  Ahmedabad Education Society (AES), a non-profit educational trust in Ahmedabad. More than 90 years old, AES was established at the behest of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, the first Home Minister of India, by illustrious leaders such as Kasturbhai Lalbhai,  the  doyen  of  the  Indian  textile  industry;  Ganesh Mavalankar, the first Speaker of the Indian Parliament; and Amrutlal Hargovandas, one of the prominent industrialists of  Ahmedabad. AES envisioned  that  the  University  would advance education not only in Gujarat but throughout India and the world. AES and its leaders have played significant roles  in  founding  national  institutions  such  as  the  Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad, the National Institute of Design, the Physical Research Laboratory, and the Centre for Environmental Planning and Technology.

The Trust is also responsible for supporting reputed institutions  such as MG Science Institute, LD Arts College, HL  College  of  Commerce,  LM  College  of  Pharmacy,  AG Teachers  College,  and  LD  Institute  of  Indology,  among many others.

AES  is  among  the  top  academic  establishments  known for  its  futuristic  perspective  and  record  of  developing best-in-class institutions both independently as well as  with  the  government,  with  its  leaders  providing  land, funding, and often management.

## Our Logo

Ahmedabad  University  defines  its  philosophical  roots  in a universal enquiry that empowers open and unbounded thought, leading humanity to prosperity. The University is informed  and  guided  by  deep  scholarship  and  multiple perspectives that enhance our vision.

The  University  logo  embodies  this  philosophy  using  a contemporary mnemonic representing the rich heritage of the city of Ahmedabad as its inspiration. The mnemonic is a stylised representation of an integral icon of the city - the stepwell, in the form of a minimalistic yet powerful graphic illustration.

At  the  structure's  core  is  the  self,  which  must  grow  with the  aim  of  transforming  society  for  the  better  while  also evolving  itself  in  the  process.  Historically,  a  stepwell  was constructed  to  sit  deep  inside  the  earth,  which  speaks to  the  University's  focus  on  enquiry  for  acquiring  deep understanding.  The  channels  and  doors  represent  the openness ingrained in the holistic philosophy of education that  Ahmedabad University embodies, from encouraging the spirit of disciplinary research to facilitating intersections that enable progress and prosperity.

It is also our pathway for building and operating a more sustainable campus.

## Green Campus initiatives

Following  are  some  of  our  efforts  to  move  toward  green  and sustainable living on Campus:

- The mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) systems of the  University  Centre  are  designed  efficiently  for  striving  to build a ZERO discharge campus (that is, reusing 100 per cent of  wastewater  to  avoid  draining  of  water  outside  campus). To  achieve  this,  we  have  installed  a  2  lakh  litre  Sewage Treatment  Plant,  thus  recycling  the  sewage  water  in  the central campus and reusing it in irrigation, flushing of toilets, and for the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) make-up water.
- The SAS, GICT buildings, and University Centre together have a 638 kW installed capacity of solar power generating 2600 units (KWh) of electricity on an average daily.
- The University Centre has electric vehicle charging stations.
- An area of 3 lakh square feet is developed as an Arboretum and lawns on the central campus, with 800 new trees planted overall.
- Six recharge wells on campus are connected to our stepwell, with work underway to connect the remaining three recharge wells.
- Sustainable farming  is  actively  pursued  on  campus  to produce fresh  seasonal  vegetables  for  consumption  at  the University food outlets.
- Food  and  garden  waste  is  converted  into  organic  manure, approximately 100 kg daily.
- Single-use plastic is strictly discouraged.

## SCHOOLS

## Amrut Mody School of Management

The  School  offers  undergraduate,  graduate,  and  doctoral programmes in management, along with short-term certificate  courses  for  working  professionals.  The  School works closely with industry and provides a balance between rigorous practice and strong conceptual education.

Its  emphasis  on  interdisciplinary  education  for  building  a broad  perspective  among  tomorrow's  leaders  through  the University-wide Foundation Programme received recognition from  the  Association  to  Advance  Collegiate  Schools  of Business  (AACSB)  International  with  the  Innovations  That Inspire Award 2023.

## Bagchi School of Public Health

The  School  aims  to  train  leaders  who  can  develop  holistic solutions  to  interdisciplinary  problems  of  the  21 st century and  to  educate  and  inspire  individuals,  communities,  and organisations.

The  School's  mission  is  to  improve  public  health  and  the environment locally, nationally, and globally through interdisciplinary collaborative education, research, and training and by developing, disseminating, and implementing sustainable evidence-based interventions, programmes, and  policies.  The  School's  initial  focus  is  on  Environmental Health  and  Analytical  Sciences,  including  Data  Science, Biostatistics,  Epidemiology,  and  executive  education.  The School's flagship programme, a PhD in Epidemiology, equips students  with  the  necessary  advanced  analytical  and  soft skills to tackle complex public health challenges.

## School of Arts and Sciences

The School of Arts and Sciences has five divisions. These are:

- Biological and Life Sciences,
- Humanities and Languages,
- Mathematical and Physical Sciences,
- Performing and Visual Arts, and
- Social Sciences.

The undergraduate programmes offer students the opportunity  to  take  courses  spanning  these  divisions  as well  as  from  other  Schools  and  Centres  at  the  University. The  School  provides  a  rigorous  foundation  for  students  in the  Major  of  their  choice.  However,  all  the  programmes  at the School emphasise interdisciplinary, research-based teaching to foster a spirit of innovation and discovery.

## School of Engineering and Applied Science

The School of Engineering and Applied Science offers undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral programmes. It emphasises project-based learning and a student-centric pedagogy.  This approach  creates  an  aptitude for design, innovation, and solving complex problems.

The  School  aims  to  inculcate  career-focused  technological expertise in its students through a hands-on approach to learning, emphasising the direct application of  skills  and  concepts.  The curriculum  is  contemporary  and  industry-oriented  and  offers specialisations ranging from Chemical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Computer Science and Engineering to Applied Physics, Applied Chemistry, and Data Science and Analytics.

## CENTRES

## Ahmedabad Design Lab

Ahmedabad Design Lab is a research, practice, and teaching space  that  helps  understand  the  challenges  facing  our society and find concomitant innovative solutions. The Lab is premised on using design as a glue for integrating systems of knowledge from multiple disciplines for problem-solving. Particularly, it addresses challenges in Ahmedabad's social, economic, and governance sectors, enabling it  to  become one of the country's most vibrant cities and the world.

## Centre for Learning Futures

The Centre offers courses in communication and workshops in  study  skills  to  help  students  present  ideas  creatively, comprehend better, and write effectively. The Centre promotes  research  innovation  in  teaching  and  learning, focusing  on  applying  knowledge  in  the  real  world.  The initiatives  for  students  at  the  Centre  include  Independent Study Period and Project-based Learning.

Ahmedabad  University  has  evolved  a  unique  framework called  Engagement  and  Application  Based  Learning  and Education or ENABLE in partnership with the Olin College of Engineering, Massachusetts, to facilitate learning by devising solutions to real-world problems.

## Global Centre for Environment and Energy

The Centre addresses research needs at the interfaces of three vital global challenges - environment, energy, and sustainability. It  aims  to  be  a  world  leader  in  research  and  education to address global challenges by interlinking policy, entrepreneurship, science,  and  technology.  It  draws  on  researchers,  teachers,  experts,  and  students  across  the  University  and  offers  them  the landscape for building core competencies to explore, innovate, integrate, and solve complex social, technical, and economic issues.

## Centre for Heritage Management

The Centre offers specialised education in heritage studies and  the professional management  of  diverse  heritage resources.  These  resources  include  but  are  not  limited  to the  management of  monuments,  crafts,  museums,  natural resources, tourism, living heritage, sustainable urban development,  and  intangible  heritage  such  as  languages, music, customs, and the performing arts. The Centre offers a  Master  of  Management  Studies  in  Heritage  Management programme, which provides theoretical models and practical tools enabling heritage professionals to benefit from some  of  the  latest  developments  in  the  areas  of  heritage management and business entrepreneurship.

## Centre for Inter-Asian Research

The  Centre  brings  together  scholars  and  practitioners  to work towards re-imagining cultural, political, and economic structures  across  the  continent.  It  seeks  to  achieve  this mandate through diverse modes of everyday living, working,  and  organising,  the  creation  of  institutions  and networks, the development and deployment of technology, economic  and  social  choice-making,  and  experimentation with  various  artistic  forms.  The  Centre  plans  to  focus  on collaborative  research  projects  across  India  and  Asia,  as well  as  interdisciplinary  research  and  teaching  across  the various Schools at Ahmedabad University. It invites students to participate in its research projects and the development of its knowledge platforms.

## VentureStudio

VentureStudio is the innovation and startup incubation hub of Ahmedabad University, established in 2011 in collaboration with  Stanford  University's  Center  for  Design  Research.  It provides  a  dynamic  platform  for  aspiring  entrepreneurs, especially students, to build, validate, and scale innovative ideas. With a focus on science and technology-led ventures, the centre offers 360° support including mentorship, prototyping infrastructure, funding opportunities, and expert networks.

The VentureStudio Fellowship Programme, open to undergraduate students of Ahmedabad University, is designed to be intensive, project-based, and multidisciplinary, providing  students  with  real-life  experience  of  starting  an innovative  business.  Besides  workshops  conducted  by  the subject  area  experts,  students  also  interact  closely  with successful entrepreneurs, investors, and mentors.

The  centre  operates  dedicated  innovation  facilities:  DST Prayas  Shala  (Engineering  Fabrication  Shop)  and  Bio-NEST (Biotechnology Lab), providing hands-on access to prototyping and bio-incubation infrastructure. Students with  promising  ideas  can  receive  grant  support  through government-backed schemes such as NIDHI-PRAYAS, NIDHI-EIR, SSIP, and more.

Whether  you  are  exploring  an  idea  or  ready  to  launch  a venture, VentureStudio  offers  students  the  environment, guidance, and resources to turn innovation into impact.

## ACADEMIC LIFE

At  Ahmedabad  University,  instead  of  focusing  on  the traditional  lecture-based  method,  our  emphasis  is  on teaching students to think for themselves, link theoretical schemes to living contexts, and bring one's concepts to enable critical thinking for solving real-world challenges. We  develop  novel,  experimental  teaching  methods  to revitalise the classroom experience for a new generation of students. To achieve this, our faculty have customised a  series  of  innovative  courses.  For  instance,  recent batches  have  been  studying  the  history  of  science through graphic novels, learning physics through constructing musical instruments, and being introduced to  forensic  biology  by  crafting  and  solving  simulated crime scenes.

Interdisciplinarity  is  both  a  core  value  and  a  guiding principle  central  to  Ahmedabad  University  since  its inception. Our vision is to create a unique system where our  students  can  actively  shape  their  intellectual  and educational trajectories by utilising the full range of the University's  offerings.  We  believe  that  instead  of  being pushed  into  narrow  and  streamlined  specialisations, students will thrive in an atmosphere where they are  exposed  to  diverse  perspectives  and  receive  a comprehensive, well-rounded education.

Positive and respectful interactions with a diverse group  of  peers  can  enhance  one's  understanding  of self and others and the ability to work in teams, as well as  inculcate  open-mindedness,  which  is  crucial  for developing innovative ideas.

We  are committed  to fostering a welcoming and respectful environment where people from diverse backgrounds can attain their full potential.

## Honours Degree

An Honours degree, rooted in liberal education, calls for deep enquiry and perspective-building that are highly valued by industry and  society.  In  every  sense,  such  a  degree  provides  a  transformational  learning  experience.  An  undergraduate  programme with honours requires academic rigour and includes a comprehensive Foundation Programme and an undergraduate thesis or  a  capstone  project  and  will  take  four  years  to  complete.  The  programme  requires  the  completion  of  173  credits*  or  more (depending on the programme). Students who aim to complete their programme earlier will require additional courses each semester, including the summer terms.

A  four-year  undergraduate  experience  ensures  eligibility  to  graduate  programmes  at  all  global  universities.  The  maximum permissible duration to complete any academic programme at the University is 10 years.

*The total number of credits are under review and are subject to change.

Undergraduate education at Ahmedabad University entails  choosing  a  programme  of  study  and  one  or  many specialisations within.

## Programmes, Major and Minor

The University offers undergraduate and integrated programmes leading to various degrees. Within each programme, a student chooses a discipline  to  specialise  in, known as a major. For instance, in a BA (Honours), a student may select  a  major  such  as  History  or  Economics.  Similarly, a  student  can  choose  a  major  in  Finance  or  Operations Management  from  the  Bachelor  of  Science  in  Management (Honours) programme.

In addition to a major, students may select an optional minor. A minor is an alternative area of specialisation outside one's major discipline, and it cannot be in the same field or be closely related to the major. The purpose of a minor is to facilitate the development of alternative areas of expertise. While declaring a  major  is  mandatory,  students  are  encouraged  to  declare a minor. Prerequisites for majors or minors may be specified regarding preparation and academic achievement.

The University also offers exceptionally well-prepared students the  flexibility  to  create  a  customised  major.  Such  a  student may opt to undertake an Interdisciplinary Major (instead of a pre-defined major) or a Self-designed Major.

Students must initiate a request for an interdisciplinary major and  defend  a  proposal  before  a  Committee  on  Individual Choice. If approved, the programme of study will be designed by  the  Committee  on  Individual  Choice  in  conjunction  with the  student.  An  interested  student  should  apply  to  a  single major  at  the  time  of  application  and  may  indicate  their interest in an Interdisciplinary Major. The student will work with the Committee on Individual Choice during their first year at the  University,  designing  the  interdisciplinary  major  and  its accompanying programme of study by the end of the second semester. (Note that this option is in addition to the pre-designed interdisciplinary majors at the University, such as Philosophy, History and Languages, or Social and Political Sciences in the Bachelor of Arts Programme, or Finance and Economics in the Bachelor of Science in Management Programme.)

## Details of the Undergraduate Curriculum

## Foundation Programme

All  students  entering  the  undergraduate  programme  go through  our  common  core,  the  Foundation  Programme,  in the  first  year.  This  builds  the  foundations  of  interdisciplinary learning  and  engagement  with  issues  of  society  through project-based  learning.  The  programme  is  built  around  six domains  that  define  contemporary  academic  and  life  skills in  the  21 st century,  areas  of  innovation  that  are  transforming the  world,  and  perspectives  that  help  students  become responsible members of any society. This includes:

| Areas of Influence   | Domains of Engagement                         |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Skills               | 1. Data science 2. Communication              |
| Perspective building | 3. Behaviour 4. Constitution and Civilisation |
| Innovation           | 5. Biology and Life 6. Materials              |

Students  develop  a  distinctive  approach  to  personal  and professional life by engaging with these domains. The foundational elements of these domains are delivered through three thematic studios, helping students imbibe a holistic and hands-on  approach  to  thinking,  enquiry,  and  action.  They learn  to  understand  multiple  issues  that  define  a  problem, learn  the  art  and  science  of  synthesis,  and  design  solutions for  the  challenges.  Every  student  participates  in  these  three studios.

The  following  are  the  three  designated  Thematic  Studios offered in 2025-26:

- Democracy and Justice
- Environment and Climate Change
- Water

In  addition  to  the  three  studios,  all  students  will  also  take  a First-year Interdisciplinary Seminar, focusing on reading, writing,  and  critical  thinking.  Offered  in  a  studio  format,  this seminar will be considered the fourth studio of the Foundation Programme.  The  student  chooses  one  course  from  a  list  of such studio courses offered in the winter semester.

The Foundation Programme will contribute 12 credits* to the total number of credits. The Foundation Programme studio on Environment  and  Climate  Change  satisfies  all  requirements for pursuing a course on the environment.

## General Education Requirements

The General Education Requirements build the breadth further while starting to understand the disciplines' methods that  help  build  intellectual  enquiry,  systems  of  knowledge, and  skills  from  various  disciplines.  It  allows  for  additional exploration  stemming  from  the  introduction  to  disciplines in  the  Foundation  Programme.  It  also  allows  the  student  to discover their passion and interests further.

The following will make up the General Education Requirements

-  at  least  one  course  of  three  credits*  from  each  of  the following areas (adding up to fifteen credits*):
- Humanities and Languages
- Social Sciences
- Mathematical and Physical Sciences
- Biological and Life Sciences
- Performing and Visual Arts

(the above courses can be in the major area)

In  addition,  students  are  expected  to  take  a  three  credit* General  Education  Requirement  in  the  Sports  and  Wellness domain during their first year. They will get to choose a sport or  activity  of  interest  from  several  options  offered  at  the University.

You need to complete twelve additional credits* from across  the  University,  including  courses  in  technology  and management.

These credits cannot be from your major area. Communication courses count towards your General Education Requirements. At  least  one  three  credit  course  in  mathematical,  statistical, or programming methods is required. A Data Science course fulfills  this,  and  all  students  must  take  it.  You  can  take  these courses  alongside  your  major  courses  or  free  electives  any semester. We encourage you to spread them throughout your years  at  the  University.  Some  majors  may  require  specific General Education courses to deepen your understanding of that discipline.

## Major Requirements

A  major  signifies  the  specialisation  area  within  a  degree (e.g.,  BA  in  History,  BTech  in  Chemical  and  Environmental Engineering, or BSM in Economics and Finance). The student can choose a major upon entering the University but has the flexibility  to  change  the  major  as  per  the  prescribed  policy. Some  Schools  may  require  more  credits  for  a  major  than the  80  credits*  mentioned  above  (in  which  case,  the  total requirements for the degree will be higher than 173 credits*). The University also admits students as Undecided - those who would like to choose a programme or major once they take some courses.

Please consult the credit requirements in the Programme and Major sections.

A Major may have the following structure:

- School Core
- Programme Core
- Major or Discipline Core
- Major or Discipline Electives
- Internships
- Undergraduate  Thesis  or  Capstone  Project  or  a  BTech Project with a Writing Component

Students can earn 12 credits* for their Undergraduate Thesis or Capstone Project, whether undertaken within or outside the University. For some projects, these credits can be spread over two semesters.

*The total number of credits are under review and are subject to change.

## Skill-based courses

These  courses  equip  students  to  learn  practical  skills  that help them secure employment after graduation. These courses equip students with competencies required by industries,  ensuring  readiness  for  a  job  and  thus  addressing industry-relevant skill gaps. These courses will be domain-specific and involve hands-on training to learn skills like programming, microscopy, etc.

## Free Electives and Minor

Free  electives  let  students  pursue  any  particular  area  in more  depth,  develop  alternate  areas  of  expertise,  or  learn more  about  many  other  interests.  They  provide  flexibility for  customising  education  at  the  University.  It  can  take  the following form:

- A student may take more electives in their area of expertise (or  a  major)  or  build  some  expertise  in  a  set  of  allied areas.  Students  may  use  their  Free  Electives  to  pursue their interests by taking courses across the University.
- Minor:  Students  can  pursue  alternate  areas  of  interest (in  addition  to  a  Major)  by  taking  a  few  set  courses  in a  different  area,  gaining  more  than  cursory  expertise in  more  than  one  subject.  A  student  may  use  the  Free Electives to help complete a Minor.

## Undergraduate Thesis/ Capstone

All  graduating  students  must  have  done  an  Undergraduate Thesis or a Capstone Project. Students can approach a faculty member within the  University  to  serve  as  a  mentor  for  their undergraduate thesis. Students with a CGPA below the cut-off must  complete  a  capstone  project.  These  projects  involve interdisciplinary groups (four to six students) collaborating to solve real-world problems that require expertise from multiple fields.

They  identify  a  problem  and  seek  guidance  from  a  faculty mentor to formulate a capstone project, which can be done across two semesters (six credits* each semester or three + nine credits*) or in a single semester.

## V Volunteerism

All students will complete 45 hours of community engagement to develop a sense of social responsibility, enhance problem-solving skills, and understand their role as engaged members of society. They must undergo a mandatory course, VOL-101,  which  is  a  graduation  requirement.  Students  are advised  to  complete  the  course  during  the  first  two  years

## Academic Advising and Programme Office

The  Undergraduate  College  and  the  various  Schools  at  the University  administer  the  programmes. ## What does the Programme Office do?

The Programme Office is the first stop for any query regarding your academic  programme.  The  Programme  Chair  and the  Programme  Manager  will  guide  you  throughout  your educational  journey  and  will  advise  you  on  several  issues, including:

- Course registration and providing advice for the planning of course credits
- Class scheduling
- Issuing various testimonials like no-objection certificates, bonafide, migration/ transference certificates, transcripts, etc., and attestation of various documents
- Registration for various government schemes for students, like distribution of electronic tablets
- Processing applications for government scholarships
- Educational verification for recruitment and further education
- Maintaining student records

## Add Week and Drop Month

The University encourages you to attend a few course sessions during  the  first  month  of  classes  to  decide  if  the  course interests you. This month is known as the Add Week and Drop Month. Add Week and Drop Month give flexibility to students who have attended the initial sessions of a course and wish to drop a course they registered for earlier and instead register for another course of their choice, if available. You are advised to attend sessions in the courses you are interested in during the Add Week and Drop Month, even if you have not registered for  them.  This  will  help  prevent  loss  of  attendance  in  case the  said  course  is  allotted  to  you  subsequently  based  on availability. Once the Add Week and Drop Month is over, you cannot drop any course you have registered for. However, if a student wishes to do so due to exceptional circumstances, they may only do so with the approval of the Programme Chair, the Associate Dean, or the Dean of the School to which they belong. The request may be approved or rejected based on the circumstances and the explanation offered by the student.

## Pre-Requisites

If  a  course  has  a  pre-requisite,  another  must  be  completed before  you  can  take  this  course.  For  example,  the  course ECO330 has a pre-requisite of ECO100 and ECO110. You must complete  both  ECO100  and  ECO110  before  you  register  for ECO330.

## Co-Requisites

If a course has a co-requisite, it means that another course(s) must be completed before or at the same time. For example, CSC203  has  a  co-requisite  of  MAT102.  You  must  complete MAT102 before you register into CSC203 or at the same time as  CSC203.  Remember,  when  you  are  registering  in  AURIS, you MUST register for the co-requisite course first (MAT102) or you will  not  meet the requisite requirements for your course (CSC203). The course registration system will not allow you to register into the course (CSC2O3) first.

## Antirequisites

If  a  course  has  an  anti-requisite, you cannot register for the course if you have already completed the listed anti-requisite, as  the  courses  contain  substantially  equivalent  content.  For example, MGT522 has an anti-requisite of MGT221. You cannot register for MGT522 if you have already completed MGT221.

## Auditing

Auditing  a  course  is  an  option  where  the  students  perform limited  work  based  on  their  agreement  with  the  Professor. You  do  not  receive  a  letter  grade  but  get  a  Satisfactory/ Unsatisfactory completion status, which does not count in the Grade Point Average or Graduation. The University encourages you  to  take  up  a  class  out  of  curiosity  and/or  interest, enhancing your knowledge or a course that is considered to be very tough. This academic exploration without the pressure of  performance or  apprehension of poor grades helps open up many avenues in higher learning, ensuring that you do not miss out on a subject of keen interest owing to the academic rigour  that  applies  in  a  course  with  credits.  Talk  to  your Faculty Advisors and Programme Chairs for more information regarding this.

## Maintaining Academic Standard

A minimum passing grade in each course is a D, with a grade point of 1.7 out of 4.00. At the same time, the overall passing requirement for the undergraduate and graduate programmes (master's) is a CGPA of 2.00 out of 4.00. A minimum CGPA of 2.7 out of 4.00 is required for doctoral programmes.

## What does an NP Grade mean?

An  NP  grade  in  a  course  indicates  that  the  student  has  not met  the  minimum  passing  requirement  in  that  course.  This has several implications as follows:

- If a student gets an NP in a course required for a certain programme, the student must repeat the same or an equivalent course and pass that course to be eligible for graduation.
- If a student gets an NP in an elective, they can repeat the same course or undertake any other course that s/ he is eligible for and complete it successfully to meet the total credit requirement for graduation.
- An NP grade in a course that is a prerequisite for an advanced level course may prevent the student from taking the advanced level course until the prerequisite one is cleared with a specified grade.

## Academic Standards and Integrity

Students must maintain the highest integrity level and abstain from academic malpractice while studying at the University. You must understand the integrity standards of the University to be aware of what constitutes fair practice in education, including but not limited to staying away from any act of copying during any mode of examination/evaluation, the act of copying homework or assignments, plagiarism of any sort, and ensuring timely submission of all assignments, project work, etc. The University shall take any corrective and/or punitive actions if any student is involved in any academic malpractice, which might also lead to dismissal from the University.

## What does an I Grade mean?

An I grade indicates 'incomplete,' meaning the student has not completed the coursework and/or the evaluation for a specific course. The I grade will remain on the grade sheet until  the  student  completes  all  the  course  requirements. A time frame may be specified to convert an I grade into a  course  grade.  If  the  student  cannot  fulfill  the  course requirements within the stipulated time, the I grade will get converted into an NP/F Grade.

An I grade has the following implications:

- The  student  may  not  be  allowed  to  register  for  an advanced level course, for which the course in which s/he obtained an I Grade is a prerequisite.
- The student may have to register for another course if  the one in which s/he has received an I grade is an elective to complete the degree requirements.

## Attendance Policy

Our academic programme relies on rigorous classroom engagement.; consequently, missed classes compromise learning not fully captured by examinations. Therefore, significant absences will result in lower grades, reflecting compromised learning. We encourage students to attend 100 per cent of the sessions. However, in case of emergencies, no penalty is imposed as long as a student attends at least 75 per cent of all sessions in a course.

However, if a student's attendance in a course falls below 75 per cent, one grade drop will be applied. Further, if the attendance falls below 60 per cent, the student will receive an 'NP' grade and have to repeat the course. This policy encourages students to maintain a high level of attendance and ensure that learning is not compromised.

## Authorised Absence

Students  may  participate  in  certain  external  events  and academic  and  co-curricular  engagements,  and  at  times, these coincide with the academic schedule at the University. These  may  be  conferences  where  students  present  papers, interviews,  or  tests  for  a  job  or  higher  studies,  fieldwork, sports events, or other competitions of high repute where our students represent the University. There is a provision to grant authorised absences to students for some sessions or a half/ full day/s, depending on their engagement and participation.

## Process Flow for How to Apply for Authorised Absence

- Students must apply in advance on AURIS under Quick Links Authorised  Absence  with  all  the  supporting  documents, such as an acceptance for a conference, sporting event, etc.,  along  with  a  supporting  letter  from  the  concerned University  authority  (professor,  Director  of  Sports)  if  the absence  is  related  to  a  University  activity  or  promoted by the University, as early as possible before the dates of absence.
- The  concerned  Programme  Office  receives  the  email intimation and application via AURIS and forwards it to the Programme Chair.
- The Chair will then relay the application with their recommendation  to  the  School  Dean.  She  may  consult others  at  the  University  to  ascertain  the  application's genuineness.
- Upon  verifying  the  application,  the  Programme  Chair will  inform the Programme Office and the student of the Dean's decision.
- If  approved,  an  email  notification  will  be  sent  to  the student,  Programme  Chair,  Programme  Manager,  and course instructors of the concerned student. The student should also intimate all the concerned instructors whose sessions they will miss.

Note that if the Authorised Absence is approved, the student will  be  marked  as  AA  for  the  missed  session,  and  this  will be  treated  as  equivalent  to  being  present  to  calculate  the number of absences.

## Reconduct of Midterm/Major Exam

When a student misses one or more evaluation components, including the mid or end-of-semester examination, and has been granted Unavoidable Absence or Authorised Absence, the instructor must provide an alternative evaluation. This could be a make-up examination, evaluation, or the redistribution of weightage from other components so the missed component does not contribute to the final grade. (The  make-up evaluation can also be given to the student when the course is offered again.) Suppose the missed evaluation component has a weightage greater than 30 per cent. In that case, the instructor should consult the relevant Programme Office and the  School-level  Examination  Committee  for  approval  of  a make-up  evaluation  or  for  dropping  the  weightage  of  that component and further processes.

For withdrawal of admission and seeking a refund of fees, the candidate  must  submit  the  original  fee  receipt,  admission offer  letter,  and  other  documents  received  at  the  time  of admission. Student/applicant must fill in the letter of request for withdrawal duly signed by them and the Parent/Guardian thereof. The letter is available at the Office of Admissions and Financial Aid and can be filled and submitted at the time of seeking withdrawal, along with the required documents.

## Refund of Fees

The fee refund will be processed per the prevailing University Grants Commission (UGC) guidelines and provisions made on the Admissions webpage.

## For Students in the Bachelor of Technology Programme

The refund of fees will be processed in accordance with the guidelines of the Admission Committee  for Professional Courses (ACPC), as specified from time to time, within the time limit they indicated. Fees shall be refunded after completion of the admission process, provided that another candidate fills the seat vacated by the student.

The  relevant  refund  amount  will  be  processed  after  the University receives the withdrawal application, and the refund shall  be  directly  credited  through  NEFT/RTGS  to  the  bank account provided by the applicant.

## For an Existing Student

A  student  who  has  enrolled  for  courses  at  Ahmedabad University and wishes to withdraw their admission must fill out the Application for Withdrawal of Admission, available online in  the  Ahmedabad  University  Resource  Information  System (AURIS). The student must take a printout of the form and get it  signed  by  the  concerned  personnel  from  the  Programme Office  for  'No  Dues'  and  submit  it  to  the  Programme  Office between  9:00  am  and  6.00  pm  on  any  working  day.  The application will be processed within 15 working days from the date of its receipt.

## Refund of Fees

The refund of fees will be processed in accordance with the University's  refund  policy.  The  refund  of  fees,  if  any,  shall  be directly credited through NEFT/RTGS only to the bank account given by the applicant at the time of application. The deposit amount will be refunded after the dues have been deducted, if any.

The refund of fees shall be made after deduction as per the following table:

## Independent Study Period

The  Independent  Study  Period,  or  ISP,  is  a  mini-term  in December wherein students engage in activities and courses that  are  not  a  part  of  the  regular  semester.  These  activities range from specially curated ISP courses, apprenticing with a faculty on the student's research project, or an independent research  project  developed  by  the  student  and  supervised by  a  faculty.  Students  earn  credits  for  this  work,  which  may be  counted  towards  the  GERS,  Free  Electives,  or  even  Major Electives. Some students take an ISP simply because they want to participate in this highly creativity-oriented and reflective activity.  The  specially  curated  ISP  courses  signify  a  unique experience  and  are  conducted  in  studio  mode.  The  student undergoes this  course  one  at  a  time  over  two  weeks.  These courses are designed around a passion, are interdisciplinary, are  experiential  and  project-based,  and  build  conceptual knowledge. ISP ends with an Expo where all course students display their work to the University community and the public.

Students  often  have  diverse  passions  but  no  means  to explore  them,  especially  when  they  are  unrelated  to  their academic specialisations. The ISP thus offers an opportunity to  explore  these  interests  beyond  the  classroom,  making learning very hands-on, collaborative, and intimate. Classrooms  become  studios,  and  thinking  out-of-the-box is  replaced  with  re-imagining  the  box.  These  courses  are offered in an eight-hour day format on 15 consecutive days, enabling  concentrated  learning  for  students  through  block courses, studio-inspired experiential courses, perspective, skill-building,  field  courses,  and  innovative  experiments  in learning.  ISP  is  an  amazingly  creative  campus  period  and embodies the Ahmedabad learning spirit.

## The Nalanda

The student-centric programme offers a unique opportunity for learners to engage closely with leading thinkers, fostering independent  thought  and  dialogue. The rules of  The  Nalanda  require  each  student  participant  to  read, reflect  on  the  subject  of  the  conversation,  and  come  to  the event with a question in mind.

Students  have  the  first  prerogative  of  engagement  with  the speaker.  The  event  ends  with  a  dinner  with  all  participants, where the conversation spills over into deeper discussions.

## Circle of Care

The Circle of Care is Ahmedabad University's peer support initiative designed to accompany students through their transition into university life. Recognising that students arrive from diverse backgrounds - with varied expectations, experiences, and levels of preparedness - the programme offers structured, intentional support during this critical phase.

Incoming students are formed into small, cross-disciplinary groups of 15-20 members. These circles are designed to help students build meaningful connections from the outset and foster a sense of community. Each circle is paired with two trained senior student mentors called Mitrs (meaning 'friends') and a Faculty Advisor, forming a close-knit support system that guides students academically and personally.

The initiative brings to life the University's vision of student development by nurturing care, connection, and responsibility. Through regular check-ins, reflective conversations, and responsive support, the Circle of Care creates a space where students feel seen, supported, and equipped to thrive.

## STUDENT SUPPORT

There are many preconditions to enable you to excel in academic life: finding suitable lodging that allows a quiet work and living environment, settling into a new rhythm, making critical decisions about your career path, and resolving any health-related issues, among others. The University provides all the guidance and support necessary to overcome these challenges, if any, and focus on your studies to attain your full potential. Some services and centres that support your development and growth are detailed below.

## Career Development Centre

The University's Career Development Centre helps prepare students for careers upon completion of their university education. It provides career counselling in areas of interest, information on various industries, and guidance to those seeking graduate education. It also facilitates psychometric tests and workshops that offer students a better sense of their strengths, development areas, and the kind of work that aligns with their innate abilities.

In addition, the Centre provides students with assistance regarding internships and the identification of job opportunities. The Centre organises several engagement forums with industry for students, such as guest lectures, workshops, seminars, conferences, and projects, building a strong and active university-industry interface.

## Placement and Internship Assistance

Internships enable students to gain practical experience, which provides a context for applying the principles learned in the classroom. You will have a chance to enhance your networking skills and build relationships within the industry. The Centre facilitates internships for students opting for this service, and you will have to abide by the internship rules stipulated for them.

While  we  offer  a  comprehensive  placement  service  through  critical  engagement  with  industry  stakeholders,  we  do  not  guarantee placements. Bona fide students registered at the University, and those who fulfil the basic eligibility criteria qualify for placement.

For more information, please write to us at careerdevelopment@ahduni.edu.in or visit the centre's office at the University Centre on the fourth floor.

## Sexual  Harassment,  Anti-Ragging,  Grievance Redressal and Disciplinary System

The University has a zero-tolerance policy on sexual harassment and ragging.

## Prevention of Sexual Harassment

Ahmedabad University enforces a stringent policy against sexual harassment,  deeming  any  level  of  such  conduct  a  punishable offense. As per the provisions of the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act 2013, an Internal Committee has been constituted whose members can be contacted for any complaints.

## Anti-Ragging Policy

The  University  has  strictly  prohibited  ragging  and  will  take  any required  actions  to  prevent  and  deter  the  recurrence  of  such incidences.  Ragging  among  students  of  any  form,  anywhere,  is prohibited and will not be tolerated. To learn more, please read the  Anti-Ragging  Policy  on  AURIS  or  our  website.  Any  student  of Ahmedabad  University  found  indulging  in  and/or  abetting  any form of ragging shall face strict disciplinary action, including the possible  debarring  of  such  students  from  taking  admission  to any institution of higher learning in the country. For concerns or complaints  regarding  ragging,  please  write  to  dean.students@ ahduni.edu.in

## THE CITY OF AHMEDABAD

Located at the heart of Gujarat, Ahmedabad is much more than a city; it's an extended classroom. Defined by an enterprising spirit, it  houses  renowned  educational  institutions  and  heritage  sites. As  India's  first  UNESCO  World  Heritage  City,  Ahmedabad  offers a  distinctive  learning  environment,  showcasing  the  country's finest  medieval  and  modern  architecture,  abundant  museums, colourful festivals, and diverse cultural activities.

This  eclectic  blend  of  tradition  and  modernity,  enhanced  by excellent  public  infrastructure,  malls,  and  multiplexes,  provides an  inspiring  backdrop  for  a  holistic  education.  The  city  brims with  opportunities  for  students  to  participate  in  intercollege competitions, enjoy music and performance festivals, and explore ancient monuments or bustling markets.

Ahmedabad is also an industrial city with a rich history in textiles, pharmaceuticals,  chemicals,  and  the  auto  and  engineering sectors. We encourage you to explore these unique parts of the city.
